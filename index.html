Project Title

Nano-Enhanced Battery Thermal Management System

Problem Statement

High-capacity lithium-ion batteries generate significant heat during operation, reducing performance and increasing the risk of thermal runaway. Effective thermal management is required to maintain battery reliability and preserve operational safety.

Research Overview

The proposed solution integrates an L-shaped Oscillating Heat Pipe (OHP) with a liquid cooling circuit to improve heat dissipation. This configuration aims to enhance thermal transfer from the battery assembly to the cooling fluid while maintaining compact system geometry.

Design Approach & Methodology

Simulation and Assembly Strategy

CAD Assembly

The SolidWorks assembly was developed to collect heat from the bottom of the batteries via collector plates, directing heat into the liquid domain through the L-shaped OHP. Cooling plates were designed to avoid direct contact with the battery cells while ensuring efficient thermal transfer.

Simulation Setup

A System Coupling module in ANSYS Workbench linked the thermal and fluid domains. This approach ensured synchronized calculation between solid conduction and fluid convection phenomena during the coupled analysis.

Thermal Analysis

Steady-state thermal analysis was conducted using copper for the OHP, aluminum for the collector and cooling plates, and lithium-ion material properties for the battery cells. Material characteristics were applied to evaluate temperature distribution and heat flux.

Fluid Flow Analysis

Fluid flow was modeled as laminar with velocities ranging from 0.1 m/s to 0.4 m/s within a 3 mm pipe. The analysis included continuity, momentum, and energy equations to capture the coupled thermal-fluid behavior of the coolant path.

Mesh Quality

Computational mesh optimization achieved an orthogonal quality of 0.797 with over 184,000 elements. This level of mesh refinement supported numerical reliability and increased confidence in the simulation results.

Results & Conclusion

CFD Findings and Performance Comparison

CFD Summary

The CFD findings indicate that the combined OHP and liquid cooling architecture improves heat dissipation and stabilizes operating temperature. The simulation results confirm the effectiveness of the proposed thermal management system under realistic flow conditions.

Fluid Comparison

Performance was compared between pure water and nanofluids at inlet temperatures of 283K, 293K, and 298K. The nanofluid case provided improved thermal stability across the evaluated temperature range.

Velocity Impact

Moderate velocities near 0.3 m/s with nanofluids exhibited stable temperature performance and prevented sudden temperature fluctuations that were more pronounced with pure water. This result supports the selection of optimized flow conditions for enhanced battery thermal control.
